DISABILITY PROGRAMMES - "Care for the Physically and mentally Chllenged"

OBJECTIVE:
  • To support and assist the care of challenged (mental and physical) individuals and their families, especially those who are poor
  • To provide persons with disability relief and rehabilitation
  • To spread awareness on causes, prevention and treatment of disability

PROGRAMMES
  • Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR) Programme: This is one of the forefront and high involvement programmes within WE CARE . Through the community based approach we work towards ensuring that the people with disability find a place within the community and become a contributing member of society. Unlike the institutional based rehabilitation1 (IBR) approach, under the CBR programme the person with disability lives within the community and is supported and accepted by the community. 1 Institutional based rehabilitation (IBR) focuses on displacing the people with disability from the community and placing them in an institution, where they are taken care of and nurtured by the staff and volunteers of the institution. Some of the activities of relief and rehabilitation work under CBR for persons with disability are as follows:
  • Health Programmes:
    • Aids and Appliances: Direct purchase, financial support or referrals to purchase aids and appliances for people with disability. Aids and appliances include wheel chairs, walking sticks for the blind, hearing aids, artificial limbs, software to support the blind on mobiles and computers, etc.
    • Medical Support Programmes: We train family members on how to care for the people with disability. The programme focuses on providing training in home based care and physiotherapy; financial support for the purchase of medicines and formal medical treatment of disability.
    • Health Camps: We conduct or sponsors health camps that focus on skin and general health screening to identity people with disability. This allows early identification of disability and enables the process of early rehabilitation. Such health camps are specially conducted in schools and in the families.
    • Nutrition Programmes: Direct purchase or financial support to purchase nutritional food items to people with disability and their families, especially those who are very poor.
  • Training Programmes:
    • Vocational Skills Training: We conduct and financially support training of people with disability in vocational skills that will lead to a suitable employment such as, computers skills, tailoring, handicrafts, beautician, etc.
    • Life Skills Training: We provide training to people with disability in life skills such as how to handle money, read the boards of buses, handle a telephone, etc.
    • Training Parents: We train parents regularly to help their children with disability to creatively deal with them. This training also focuses on becoming aware of the opportunities available in society for their children.
  • Education Programmes:
    • Home Based Education Programme: We provide financial and technical assistance to parents of children with disability, to support their home based education. We also provide finances to support the nutrition needs of these children.
    • Inclusive Education Programme: We extend support to the parents of children with disability. Our support is extended by helping them interact with educational institutions within their locality, to ensure that these disabled children get admission into local schools and can study with normal children. These children are also provided additional support under other education or non education programmes
  • Enable Governmental Aid:
    • Doctor's certificates: Once the people with disability are identified, we assist, co-ordinate and support them in getting a medical certificate from a recognized government doctor. This certificate is a mandatory document to be submitted to avail all government or non-government facilities.
    • Referral Services: We refer people with disability to various government and non-government agencies so that they can avail of the facilities and programmes. This allows people with disability to enrol and secure various benefits given by the government agencies such as scholarships for students, pension, bus concessions, train pass, aids and appliances, jobs, etc.

PROGRAMME EXECUTION:
  • WE CARE executes this programme with the help of a specific group of staff. The staff members are professionally trained.
  • WE CARE creates a "Target Area" within which they focus and direct most of their programmes for the challenged. The target area is limited to the residents living within the geographical boundaries of the southern district in Bangalore, Karnataka.
  • WE CARE also conducts surveys within the target area to identify people with disability. Information is also solicited from government, semi-government and other NGO's working within the area. During the survey an assessment is made to identify the needs of the people with disability.
  • Netwroking with other NGO's is an important aspect of the CBR approach. Since no single agency has all the resources available, we need to join hands with each other and share our experiences and expertise so that the people with disability will get the maximum benefit.
  • WE CARE also caters to people with disability who approach us directly from outside the target area. Others are also referred to us by the public, our donors and benefactors, hospitals, other NGOs, schools and education institutions.
  • The CBR programme for people with disability is supported substantially with the assistance of donations from AIFO, Bologna, Italy and Amici di Raoul Follereau, Bangalore. All disability programmes are funded by the donations made by donors to the WE CARE trust.
